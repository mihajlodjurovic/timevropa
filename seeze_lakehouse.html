seeze.ai

The question your stack structurally cannot ask.
Every pricing tool answers “what is this car worth?” None of them answers “why isn't it selling, and what would it take to find out?” — because that question runs backward, and SQL only runs forward. This is a reasoning engine that runs both ways, over the entire US market, in your browser.
Step 1 · The corpus

01Build the US market

Synthesized to the real shape: ~50,000 dealer rooftops (≈15K franchised, ≈35K independent — a fixed count, not a fraction of the data), 6–8M live listings, and the event table that dwarfs both — VDP views, leads, price cuts, sales, ~40 per listing. Events are clustered by listing, the way a real lakehouse partitions them, so a question about one car walks a contiguous slice instead of scanning the market.

Step 3 · What every tool does

03Price it

The forward question — what is it worth? — is the one the whole industry already answers. We answer it too, but note what it costs: we touch the car's own history and its comp set. The conventional path scans the market.








  
Step 4 · The question with no answer today

  
04Why that price? 

  
Ask any pricing tool why and it shows you the number again, prettier. The
  number is a conclusion; a conclusion has a derivation. We walk it: which comps set the market,
  which of them actually sold, at what price, after how many days. This is the conversation a
  used-car manager has with a GM, and no system in the stack can hold up its end.

  
  







  
Step 5 · Where the models get you hurt

  
05What if we cut 8%? 

  
A predictive model will always answer this — fluently, instantly, and with no
  idea whether the answer is licensed. Our engine checks the evidence first. In a liquid segment the
  elasticity is established and it returns a scoped interval. In a thin segment it refuses, and
  tells you why refusing is the profitable move.

  
  







  
Step 6 · The whole point

  
06Why isn't it selling? 

  
Here is a question your stack does not merely answer badly — it cannot represent
  it. When the data can't settle it, SQL returns an empty result and a model returns a confident
  guess. We return a priced experiment. The query leaves the database, tells you exactly what
  evidence would close the gap, what each channel costs, and which one to buy first.

  
  







  
Step 7 · Why this is buildable, not aspirational

  
07The cost of asking

  
Directed questions cost what the answer costs, not what the market
  costs. That is the entire engineering thesis, and it is why this runs in a browser tab instead of a
  warehouse cluster.

seeze.eu

The question your inventory stack structurally cannot ask.
Every pricing tool answers "what is this car worth?" None of them answers "where is the profit hiding, and why am I not capturing it?" — because that question runs backward, and SQL only runs forward. This is a reasoning engine that runs both ways, over 1M real European listings, in your browser.
Step 1 · Compile & Load

01Build the European index

First compilation reads europe_merged.history_listings from MongoDB, normalizes extracted_make/model, builds CSR indexes, and packs everything into a compact binary format (~45 MB). Subsequent loads skip straight to the browser — zero-copy TypedArrays, the entire 1M market in your tab. No LLM, no server queries after load.

Step 3 · What every tool does

03Price it

The forward question — what is it worth? We compare actual price to predicted_price and the segment market average. This is the question the industry already answers — but note what it costs to do it our way vs. a full scan.








  
Step 4 · The question with no answer today

  
04Why that price? 

  
The number is a conclusion; a conclusion has a derivation. We walk it:
  which comps define this segment, what's the profit distribution, where does this unit sit in
  the spread? This is the conversation a dealer has with a buyer — and no system holds up its end.

  
  







  
Step 5 · Where the models get you hurt

  
05What if we priced at predicted? 

  
A predictive model will always answer this — fluently, instantly.
  Our engine checks the evidence first. In a liquid segment the elasticity is established.
  In a thin segment it refuses and tells you why refusing is the profitable move.

  
  







  
Step 6 · The whole point

  
06Find the best deals in Europe 

  
Instead of asking about ONE car, we ask about ALL of them: which listings
  have the highest profit potential right now? This runs backward through the index —
  and returns the answer before a conventional query finishes loading the first page.

  
  







  
Step 7 · Why this is buildable

  
07The cost of asking

  
Directed questions cost what the answer costs, not what the market
  costs. That is the entire engineering thesis, and it is why this runs in a browser tab.
